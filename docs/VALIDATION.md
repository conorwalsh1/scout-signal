# Phase 12 — First User Validation

Use this checklist after deployment to validate the product with real users. The goal is to learn whether the signal intelligence is useful and to get the first paying customer.

## 12.1 Show to real recruiters

- [ ] Identify 3–5 recruiters (independent or small agency) who source their own clients.
- [ ] Share the app (or a demo with seed data) and a short context: “Hiring-signal dashboard so you know which companies to pitch.”
- [ ] Let them use it: sign up, open dashboard, open a company, save one.

## 12.2 Observe and ask

Track answers and reactions to:

- Do they **trust the signal explanations**? (e.g. “4 jobs posted recently”, “Hiring spike detected”)
- Do the **surfaced companies feel worth pitching**? (relevance, recency, quality)
- Do they **understand the score**? (what 75 vs 35 means, why a company is ranked higher)
- What’s missing or confusing? (filters, categories, regions, data freshness)

## 12.3 Improve signal clarity before new features

- If explanations or scores are unclear, refine copy and UI (explanations, score badge, empty states) before adding connectors or automation.
- Do not add outreach automation, more sources, or extra product surface until signal quality and clarity are validated.

## 12.4 Milestone: First paying stranger

- [ ] One person who is not a friend/family pays for Pro (or starts a trial that converts).
- Use that as the signal to invest further in connectors, scheduling, and polish.

## Success criteria (from IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN)

- 5 recruiters say the signals are useful.
- 1 recruiter uses it repeatedly.
- 1 stranger pays.
- System collects clean historical signal data (via your workers and/or seed).

## When validation is done

- Update **progress.txt**: mark Phase 12 complete, note what you learned and what you’ll improve next.
- Plan the next iteration (e.g. real career-page connector, more signal types, or UX improvements) based on feedback, not assumptions.
